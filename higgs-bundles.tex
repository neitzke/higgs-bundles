\section{Syllabus}

Moduli spaces of Higgs bundles are extremely 
geometrically rich, and yet reasonably 
accessible, spaces. 
The aim of the course is to learn as much as we can about 
the topology and geometry of these spaces. One 
idiosyncratic point is that we will focus more than 
usual on the fact that these spaces are \ti{\hk} 
(as opposed to merely holomorphic symplectic).

A very rough list of topics:
\begin{itemize}
  \item speedy review of \kahler geometry
  \item basic notions of \hk geometry, hyperholomorphic bundles, twistor spaces
  \item \hk geometry of $\R^4$, $\R^3 \times S^1$, $\R^2 \times T^2$, $T^* \C\PP^1$
  \item coadjoint orbits?
  \item moduli space of solutions of Nahm equations?
  \item moduli space of Higgs bundles for the group $G = U(1)$
  \item definition of moduli spaces of semistable $G$-Higgs bundles
  \item Hitchin's integrable system
  \item SYZ mirror symmetry?
  \item nonabelian Hodge theorem
  \item \hk structure on moduli of Higgs bundles
  \item Morse function, computation of Betti numbers
  \item asymptotic construction of harmonic bundles
  \item asymptotic construction of \hk metric
  \item abelianization, cluster coordinates?
  \item exact description of \hk metric?
  \item Hausel's purity conjecture?
\end{itemize}
